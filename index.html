About Me

Environmental Engineer & Ecologist.

I am currently a first year master's student in Environmental Management at Duke University with concentrations in Terrestrial and Freshwater Systems and Environmental Analytics and Modeling. I recently finished my bachelor's degree in Environmental Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University where I focused on sustainable and ecologically-minded engineering practices. Growing up in southwest Virginia, I was surrounded by the Blue Ridge Mountains and the New River. The environment was a core part of my upbringing and continues to motivate me in my career and personal life. I want to work to protect our freshwater resources, including rivers, lakes, and wetlands, with the landscapes of Southern Appalachia holding a special place in my heart.

I have professional experience working as an engineering intern and in water quality monitoring. I am currently a Stanback fellow working with The Nature Conservancy on river water quality impacts of Hurricane Helene in the Blue Ridge region of western North Carolina. In my engineering portfolio, you can find environmental and engineering projects I have done throughout my education. In my artistic portfolio, you can find my work in theatre, photography, and woodworking. As an artist, in an addition to an engineer, I am always looking for ways to combine creativity with environmental and sustainability solutions. Please don’t hesitate to reach out if you have any questions or are interested in working together.
